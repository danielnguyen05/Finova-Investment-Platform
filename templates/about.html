FINOVA is a personal finance platform designed to help users construct optimized investment portfolios using data-driven insights. Our portfolio-building system is rooted in Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) and Linear Programming (LP), allowing for the maximization of expected returns while managing risk effectively.

How Portfolio Building Works

Our portfolio construction follows Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT), which emphasizes maximizing expected return for a given level of risk. Risk is measured using beta a metric that quantifies a stock’s volatility relative to the overall market. To optimize the risk-return balance, we employ Linear Programming to determine the best asset allocation that maximizes expected return while ensuring the portfolio’s beta remains below the user’s risk threshold.

Optimization Process

The optimization process consists of three main steps:

  • Defining Constraints – The total portfolio weights must sum to 100%, and the total portfolio beta must stay below the threshold defined by the selected risk preference.
  • Objective Function – The system maximizes expected return, calculated as the weighted sum of the expected returns of the selected stocks.
  • Risk Calibration – The LP model adjusts stock allocations dynamically, ensuring that high-beta stocks are balanced by more stable assets to maintain the desired risk level.

This method ensures efficient capital allocation optimizing the risk-return tradeoff by systematically distributing capital among stocks in a way that preserves stability while seeking growth

Company Selection & Diversification

Currently, the portfolios are built from a preselected set of stocks chosen based on liquidity, market capitalization, and sector representation. This approach ensures that the optimization process remains efficient while avoiding computational complexity.

Sector diversification is a critical component of portfolio stability. Investing across multiple industries helps mitigate risk by reducing exposure to sector-specific downturns. A well-diversified portfolio balances exposure across technology, healthcare, finance, and consumer goods, ensuring that external economic shocks do not significantly impact overall performance.

  • NFLX (Netflix) - Media/Entertainment

Future Enhancements

Currently, our model uses a predefined selection of companies, but we are developing a customizable portfolio-building system where users can select their own stock while still benefiting from our LP-based optimization framework.

The planned process will include:

  1. User Stock Selection – Users will choose stocks from a broader universe based on their investment preferences.
  2. Risk & Return Analysis – The system will analyze stock beta, expected return, and covariance to assess their suitability.
  3. Optimization Algorithm Execution – The LP solver will calculate the best weight allocations for the selected stocks, ensuring maximum returns within the user's risk tolerance.
  4. Real-Time Visualization – A dynamic UI will provide instant feedback on how changes in stock selection affect risk-return profiles.

By integrating user-defined stock choices while maintaining the mathematical rigor of our LP model, this future update will enhance portfolio flexibility, allowing for more personalized investment strategies.
